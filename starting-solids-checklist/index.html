Starting Solids Readiness Checklist

A quick gut-check for calmer, more confident starting solids.

1

Baby Readiness

  • Baby is around 4-6 months old
  • Baby can sit upright with support
  • Baby has steady head and neck control
  • Baby brings hands or toys to mouth
  • Baby shows curiosity around food
2

Mealtime Setup

  • Baby has a safe, supported seat
  • I know safe shapes and textures
  • I know gagging vs. choking
  • I can stay calm if baby spits food out
  • I know early meals are for learning, not just intake
3

Signs You’d Benefit From Support

  • Baby is old enough to start, but you still feel unsure
  • Allergens feel confusing or scary
  • You’re not sure which feeding tools are actually necessary
  • You feel pressure to buy all the plates, spoons, cups, and gadgets
  • You wonder if baby needs separate meals or purees
  • You worry about gagging, choking, or doing something wrong
  • You’re not sure how much food to offer or whether baby is eating enough
  • You want a calm plan instead of piecing together advice online
  • You want someone to walk you through the process step by step

If you checked one or more boxes in the last section, you don’t need to buy your way into feeling ready with more feeding tools. You deserve a clear plan and support that helps you feel calm at the table.
